# Fascia Equation — Toroidal Connective Tissue

The carousel's connective tissue. The point equations (g_trained, D, A)
operate at individual slides. The fascia equation operates on the
SURFACE between slides — what makes the reader feel that slide 4
connects to slide 7, that the carousel is one body, not ten organs.

## The Toroid IS the Fascia

Two dimensions transmit force:

- **theta (major radius)** = narrative sequence. Through-line from
  slide to slide. "First this, then this, therefore this."
- **phi (minor radius)** = thematic depth. Cross-connections between
  non-adjacent slides. Slide 2's claim echoed in slide 7's finding.
  A motif planted in slide 1 that blooms in slide 9.

When phi = 0, the toroid collapses to a ring. Content flows in
sequence but nothing resonates across distance. This was v9's failure.

## Force Equation

```
F(i->j) = |Dbelief(i->j)| x T(i) x V(i) x Z(i,j)
```

- **Dbelief(i->j)**: How much the reader's model shifts between
  slide i and slide j. If belief doesn't change, force = 0.
- **T(i)**: Tensegrity propagation — how far the restructuring
  spreads beyond the immediate point of contact.
- **V(i)**: Vulnerability factor — genuine + performed. The dual
  mechanism that creates trust surface for the discovery.
- **Z(i,j)**: Impedance match — vocabulary, reference frame,
  emotional register. Mismatch = reflection. Match = absorption.

## Dbelief Decomposition

```
Dbelief = B x A x Q x E x D
```

- **B (commitment)**: How deeply the reader committed to their
  prediction before disruption. The Lock. Higher B = deeper flip.
- **A (acuteness)**: Sharpness of disruption. Scalpel, not hammer.
  Specific enough that the reader can't dismiss as general.
- **Q (quench speed)**: Rate of cooling after disruption. Too slow:
  rationalizes back. Too fast: brittle. Optimal: restructures.
- **E (error gradient)**: Directional information from disruption.
  Without gradient, disruption is noise. WHICH DIRECTION to update.
- **D (discovery mode)**: The Mobius factor. How much the insight
  feels like the reader's own discovery vs being told. Maximum when
  creator and reader are on the same cognitive surface.

If ANY sub-factor = 0, the whole force = 0. All five are multiplicative.

## Total Force

```
F_fascia(i) = F_theta(i->i+1) + S F_phi(i<->j)
```

Sequential tension + thematic resonance from all cross-connected slides.

Carousel strength = weakest link:

```
F_carousel = min(F_fascia(i)) for all i
```

If ANY transition has zero force, the reader drops. Find it. Fix it.

## Dual Scale

**MACRO** (between slides):

- theta = narrative through-line connecting slide N to N+1
- phi = thematic echoes connecting slide N to distant slides

**MICRO** (within each slide):

- theta = text content (the sequential argument)
- phi = animation (the thematic resonance — visual demonstrates concept)

A slide where text and animation serve the same concept has high
micro-phi. A slide where animation is decorative has micro-phi = 0.
The Veritasium test ("describe it — would they understand the
mechanism?") IS a test for micro-phi.
