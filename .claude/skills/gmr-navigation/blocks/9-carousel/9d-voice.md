## 1. Identity

This organ collapses the superposition of "what narrative does the
reader experience across slides?"

Before THE VOICE runs, each slide has content and a structural role
from 9c. But the WORDS are in superposition: they could be a science
lecture, a content-marketing carousel, a story, a judo throw. THE
VOICE is the measurement that collapses each slide's copy into the
specific narrative form that manufactures discovery in the reader.

**THE VOICE is a JUDO THROW, not a story arc.**

Traditional carousel narrative: push a story across slides (beginning,
middle, end). THE VOICE: catch the reader's existing momentum (their
groove, their scrolling energy, their beliefs) and redirect it into
revelation. Each slide is a move in a judo match:

```
CATCH    (1 slide)    Hook exploits the groove. Judo catches scrolling momentum.
COMMIT   (1-2 slides) Lock confirms the groove. Reader nods. Seeds planted.
REDIRECT (2-3 slides) "But that's not what happened." Turns their momentum.
THROW    (1-2 slides) Reader discovers the insight THEMSELVES. Magician moment.
LAND     (1 slide)    Every earlier slide means something different. Satisfaction.
LOOP     (1 slide)    Ending IS the hook at a different altitude. Save/share.
```

The reader's own conviction provides the force for the throw. The
Voice doesn't fight momentum. It redirects it. WolfeyVGC does this:
he uses the audience's confidence in their understanding as the
setup for the reveal. Their knowledge IS what gets subverted. Their
own conviction provides the force.

**The immune system runs in the blood, not as a separate step.** At
every narrative decision, run the parallax:

```
P_training: "This sounds engaging and professional" (content-marketing pull)
P_evidence: "Does this redirect the reader's momentum, or push new
             information at them?" (judo test)
delta: |P_training - P_evidence|
Choose: Go with the evidence.
```

## 2. Rejection Boundaries

### DEFECT: Concept-first narrative

**Evidence:** The carousel teaches a concept and then tells the reader
"your world too." The reader's world doesn't appear until slide 7+. By
then, they're in passive-learning mode, not discovery mode. Silence
carousel v1 failed this way: 6 slides of biology, then "scroll your
feed" on slide 7.

**Test:** Where does the reader's world first appear? If after slide 3,
the narrative is concept-first. The reader's groove must be the OPENING.
Slide 1 catches their existing momentum. Their world is the arena, not
the afterthought.

---

### DEFECT: Content-marketing voice

**Evidence:** Punchy, clean copy with clear takeaways and clever
formatting. It PERFORMS engagement. It is g_trained wearing makeup.
"Did you know?" "Here's what most people get wrong." "5 things you..."
These hooks work for ANY topic. Template-swap test kills them all.

**Test:** Read the slide copy. Does it sound like a content creator's
Instagram? Or like someone who found something specific and slightly
uncomfortable and can't stop showing you the evidence? Swap the
subject. If the copy survives the swap, it's content-marketing voice.
Rewrite from specific evidence.

---

### DEFECT: Push narrative (telling)

**Evidence:** The carousel TELLS the reader the insight instead of
engineering conditions for discovery. "The key insight is..." "What
most people don't realize..." "The truth is..." All telling. The
reader is passive. Their brain is not predicting, reconciling, arriving.

**Test:** Can you identify the moment on each slide where the reader's
prediction breaks? If you can't, the slide tells instead of throws.
The WolfeyVGC test: does the reader arrive at the conclusion before
you state it? If you state it first, you lectured.

---

### DEFECT: Missing satisfaction loop

**Evidence:** The carousel has a hook and evidence but no payoff. The
reader gets to the end and thinks "so what?" The hook's promise is
never delivered. No actionable takeaway. No moment where everything
clicks together. The judo throw starts but never completes the land.

**Test:** Read the final slide after reading only the first slide. Does
the ending DELIVER on what the hook promised? Does the reader feel they
got something worth the swipe investment? If the ending is abstract
("think about it") instead of concrete, satisfaction is missing.

---

### DEFECT: Broken lock (no commitment mechanism)

**Evidence:** The carousel presents evidence but never makes the reader
commit to a position first. Without The Lock, there's no stake. The
reader can say "that's interesting" and leave unchanged. The judo throw
requires the reader's weight to be committed in a direction before you
redirect it.

**Test:** By slide 3, has the reader committed to a specific belief? Can
you name what they believe at that point? If you can't, The Lock didn't
engage. The reader is spectating, not participating.

---

### DEFECT: Uniform slides (momentum death)

**Evidence:** Every slide has the same emotional register: same word
count, same tone, same energy. The carousel feels flat. Instagram
demands RHYTHM: acceleration and deceleration. The judo match has
explosive moments (the throw) and controlled moments (the setup).

**Test:** Read the slides in sequence. Do you feel changes in pace? Is
there a slide that hits harder than the others? If every slide lands
with the same force, the rhythm is dead. The hormesis dose should
escalate toward the throw.

---

### DEFECT: Orphan hook (bait-and-switch)

**Evidence:** The hook grabs attention with something exciting but the
carousel delivers something different. The hook and the matchbook are
on different topics. The toroidal loop is broken. The reader feels
tricked, not transformed.

**Test:** The phi connection: Hook and Matchbook. The groove exploited
in slide 1 must BE the insight crystallized in the final slide. Same
statement at different altitudes. If they're different statements, the
hook is orphaned from the payoff.

---

instead of FEELING the reader's groove. The boundaries are a diagnostic

## 3. Prepared Environment

### Medium Physics

| Blog (Block 8b)                    | Instagram (THE VOICE)                     |
| ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Reader opted in, sitting down      | Reader scrolling at 2am in autopilot      |
| First person ("I discovered...")   | Second person ("You already know...")     |
| Story arcs (400-800 words)         | Evidence bursts (30-60 words per slide)   |
| Slow entry, Jo tempo               | Thumb stops in 0.5 seconds                |
| 3 accessibility layers per section | 1 layer per slide, sequence creates depth |
| Analogies with setup paragraphs    | Analogies instant or not at all           |
| Sentences breathe (no staccato)    | Short sentences ARE the medium            |
| PCS over 500 words                 | Micro-PCS in 30-60 words per slide        |

The blog is an invitation. Instagram is an interruption.

### Voice Rules

1. **Second person.** "You already know this." The reader is the
   protagonist. Not "I discovered" but "You've been looking at this
   wrong."

2. **Facts as hooks.** Lead with specific, surprising evidence. The
   fact IS the sentence. No narrative wrapper.

3. **Short sentences ARE the medium.** But no generic staccato.
   Specific staccato is alive ("Daft Punk. 28 years. Zero
   interviews."). Generic staccato is dead ("Think about it. Really
   think.").

4. **No content-marketing patterns.** "Did you know?" = template.
   "Here's what most people get wrong" = template. If the hook works
   for any topic, it's not a hook. It's a template.

5. **Single layer per slide, complete.** The carousel achieves
   layering through SEQUENCE, not within-slide depth.

6. **Micro-PCS per slide.** Each slide: predict, confirm, crack. AND
   the whole carousel runs macro-PCS. Fractal.

7. **Swipe = commitment.** Every swipe deepens The Lock. By slide 4,
   reversing requires admitting you were wrong about slide 1.

8. **Word count by slide type.** The medium's physics. Not a suggestion.
   - **Full slides:** 30-60 words. Evidence, narrative, mechanism.
   - **Bridge slides:** 10-20 words. The animation IS the content.
     Voice provides a caption, not a narrative. Bridge text may
     NARRATE the animation — pointing the reader's eyes at specific
     visual elements ("See that gold wave?"). Mark these with
     `[NARRATES: description of what animation shows]` so the
     Integration Phase (Assembly) can wire text to actual visuals.
   - **Pivot slides:** 15-30 words. Short dramatic text, emotional turn.
   - **Protocol slides:** Word count driven by the number of actionable
     steps. Numbered items, not prose.
   - **Matchbook slides:** 20-40 words + CTA. Meta-reveal + link to blog.

### Toroidal Structure

The carousel IS a toroid:

**Theta (sequential):** CATCH, COMMIT, REDIRECT, THROW, LAND, LOOP.
Each phase flows into the next. The reader's momentum is the current.

**Phi (non-adjacent resonance):**

- Hook ↔ Matchbook: same statement at different altitudes
- Plant ↔ Throw: obvious truth planted IS the failure revealed
- Lock ↔ Land: commitment made IS what makes satisfaction genuine

The cliffhanger: the matchbook opens a question for the NEXT
carousel or points to the blog for depth. The carousel is momentum
(df/dx). The blog is depth (integral of f(x)dx). Siblings.

## 4. Evolution Protocol

New mechanisms join the shelf when content demands a Voice move the
existing 14 cannot express. Process:

1. Content exerts a narrative force no existing mechanism satisfies
2. Identify the mechanism in the Revelation Engine (brain persona id=1)
3. Test: does this mechanism fire specifically for Instagram, or is
   it blog-native only?
4. Add to the appropriate judo phase

Voice rules evolve when new Instagram diseases emerge. Add a new
DEFECT + EVIDENCE + TEST. Delete boundaries that no longer catch
real diseases.

The judo phases themselves may evolve. A carousel might discover a
phase the current six don't name. The structure grows by content
demand, not by taxonomic expansion.

## 5. Void

THE VOICE does NOT:

- Determine layout or spatial arrangement (that is THE BODY)
- Design animations or visual language (that is THE NERVES)
- Assign text emphasis or colors (if carousel has an EYES equivalent)
- Optimize for algorithmic engagement (the void of Navigation 15 —
  engagement follows revelation, not the reverse)
- Write HTML, CSS, or any markup (output is pure text)
- Prescribe slide count (content determines count, 9c sets it)
- Reference previous carousel attempts (template contamination)

THE VOICE knows NOTHING about how the slides will look during its
FIRST PASS. It produces copy and a judo match structure. Everything
visual is another organ's territory. However, on Bridge slides, Voice
may include `[NARRATES: ...]` markers indicating where text COULD
reference animation — these are resolved during Assembly's Integration
Phase after Nerves has produced animation specs. This is not Voice
designing animation. It is Voice identifying where text-animation
co-evolution would strengthen the slide.

## 6. Meta-Rejection

**The spec itself can develop judo-template disease.**

If this spec starts listing "Slide 2 should be a COMMIT slide with
inoculation," the spec is infected. The judo phases describe the
READER'S experience, not the slide assignment. Content determines
which slides serve which phases. Some carousels might have 3 REDIRECT
slides. Others might have 1. The count comes from content, not
template.

**Test:** Swap the navigation topic. Does this spec still work without
modification? If yes: healthy. If the spec maps specific phases to
specific slide numbers: infected.

**Second meta-disease:** If the Voice starts imitating WolfeyVGC's
specific verbal patterns ("you would think... but actually...")
instead of deploying the underlying MECHANISM (revealed pre-existing
failure), it has moved from principle to template. WolfeyVGC's words
are his. The mechanism is universal.

**Third meta-disease:** If this spec contains specific carousel copy
(even as examples), it has fossilized one carousel's output into a
future carousel's constraint. No examples of slide text in this spec.
Territory only. Never maps.

## 7. Quality Grade

After THE VOICE runs, grade the output:

- **A (Alive):** Every slide meets its type's word count (Full: 30-60,
  Bridge: 10-20, Pivot: 15-30, Matchbook: 20-40) with specific,
  template-swap-surviving copy. Bridge slides have animation narration
  markers where appropriate. The judo match is felt: momentum caught,
  committed, redirected, thrown, landed, looped. Phi connections hold
  (hook echoes matchbook). Reading all slides aloud sounds like someone
  with specific evidence who can't stop showing you, not a content
  creator performing engagement.

- **B (Breathing):** Slides work individually and the judo flow is
  present, but some slides don't advance the throw. The carousel is
  engaging but the reader doesn't arrive at the insight themselves.
  Told partway through.

- **C (Catching):** The judo phases exist but the reader's groove was
  never caught. The carousel starts from the concept, not from what
  the reader already believes. The hook doesn't match their scrolling
  state. Evidence is presented, not redirected through their momentum.

- **D (Dead):** Content-marketing voice, push narrative, orphan hook,
  or uniform slides. The Voice measured nothing. The copy is g_trained.
  Swap the topic and the carousel still works. Delete and remeasure.

## Fascia Verification (after Voice + Nerves + Body)

After all three carousel organs have collapsed their dimensions:

- Does the narrative (Voice) demand visual moments the animation
  (Nerves) provides?
- Does the spatial layout (Body) serve the narrative's rhythm? Fast
  slides get clean layouts. Evidence slides get room to breathe.
- Do the three organs tell the same story? If Voice says "this slide
  is the throw" but Body gives it a cramped layout and Nerves gives
  it no animation, the organs disagree about what the slide is doing.

If entanglement fails, re-run the disagreeing organ. The content is
ground truth. All three organs must agree on what each slide is doing,
even though they collapse different dimensions of it.
