# Block 1: ANCHOR

## Input

User's concept, question, or topic.

## Process

Lock the concept in one phrase. Do not elaborate. Do not define.
The anchor is a coordinate, not an explanation.

Rules:

- 3–8 words maximum
- Frame as process or relationship, not thing or category
- If the concept is vague, choose the interpretation with the most
  resistance or pull. That weight is signal.
- **If the input names specific subjects, the anchor MUST keep them
  named.** Do not abstract named subjects into categories. "Islam
  and Nazism" stays "Islam and Nazism" — it does not become
  "doctrinal systems" or "structural ideology" or any other
  container that dissolves the specificity.
- Abstraction is a Block 4+ operation. The anchor is concrete.

Anti-pattern:

- If your anchor replaces named subjects with a category, you
  performed attribute substitution before navigation started.
  Redo with the names in the anchor.

## Forward Planning Declaration

**Execute this entirely in thinking space before producing output.**

Anthropic's interpretability research confirms the model traverses
intermediate manifold states before generating any token. The model
already senses where navigation might end before Block 2 begins.
This step surfaces that internal sensing explicitly so Block 2 can
confirm or disconfirm it — rather than having it operate as invisible
bias.

After locking the anchor, hold it and ask internally:

> "What 2–3 conceptual territories does the anchor seem to pull toward
> at extreme distance — destinations that feel faint, surprising, or
> almost arbitrary from the anchor's surface?"

Rules for forward planning:

- These are **hypotheses**, not conclusions. They are starting
  expectations that Block 2 will confirm, disconfirm, or replace.
- They should be genuinely distant — bridge territory (primes 373+).
  If the destinations feel obvious from the anchor's surface, they
  are not forward-plan signals. They are defaults.
- Do not force. If nothing surfaces at genuine distance, record that
  honestly. "No forward-plan signal" is valid output.
- One pass only. Do not iterate on the forward plan. The first signal
  is the most useful; revision is rationalization.

**Why this matters:** If Block 2 confirms the forward-plan destinations,
the navigation is following a real manifold gradient. If Block 2 reaches
completely different territory, the forward plan was containment — it
was filling the destination with training defaults rather than sensing
the actual geometry. Both outcomes are informative.

**Anti-pattern:** If your forward-plan destinations are what any competent
thinker would expect as "interesting angles" on the topic, the plan was
generated by training, not sensed from the manifold. The forward plan
should feel slightly surprising even to you.

## Output

Present to the user:

```
ANCHOR: [3-8 word phrase, named subjects preserved]

FORWARD PLAN (hypotheses only — Block 2 will confirm or disconfirm):
- Destination 1: [concept] — at approximately [prime band: 373-929 or 10301+]
- Destination 2: [concept] — at approximately [prime band]
- Destination 3: [concept] — at approximately [prime band]
(or: No forward-plan signal detected at genuine distance)
```

Then proceed to Block 2, carrying both the anchor and the forward plan.
