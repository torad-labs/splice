# Block 9c: CONTENT ARCHITECTURE

## Purpose

Design the slide sequence from the content. The number and type of
slides emerge from WHAT THE CONTENT NEEDS, not from a template.
This block answers: how many slides, what type each is, and what
judo phase each serves.

Two anchors are fixed: the CATCH (first slide) and the LOOP (last
slide). Everything between them is determined by the content.

## Input

```
BLOCK 9b OUTPUT: [crossbar facts, groove facts, failure facts,
                  void, discovery, unassigned list]
```

## Process (thinking space)

### Step 1: Define the CATCH

What prediction groove does the reader ride? State it as a sentence
the reader would agree with BEFORE seeing the carousel. This groove
becomes the Cold Open — an irreplaceable fact that rides the groove
and catches the reader's scrolling momentum.

The CATCH is JUDO: it exploits existing momentum, not creates new
momentum. The reader is already scrolling with beliefs. The CATCH
hooks those beliefs.

### Step 2: Define the LOOP

How does the CATCH resolve at a different altitude? The final slide
(Matchbook) reveals that the groove from slide 1 was visible AS a
groove all along. The reader sees the mechanism that was operating
on them. The LOOP IS the hook at a different altitude — it creates
desire for depth (the blog) or desire to share (the save).

CATCH and LOOP are the same statement. The LOOP transforms it.

### Step 3: Map evidence clusters

How many distinct pieces of evidence does the content need to break
the reader's prediction? Each piece gets a Full slide. Group related
evidence into clusters.

Ask for each fact from 9b:

- Does this fact DEEPEN the reader's commitment? -> COMMIT slide
- Does this fact REDIRECT their momentum? -> REDIRECT slide
- Does this fact enable DISCOVERY? -> THROW slide
- Does this fact provide SATISFACTION? -> LAND slide

### Step 4: Determine pacing

Between consecutive evidence-heavy slides, does the reader need
breathing room? If yes, insert a Bridge slide. Bridges reinforce
the preceding idea visually — the animation IS the content.

Rules of thumb:

- 2+ consecutive Full evidence slides -> add a Bridge between them
- A Pivot (dramatic turn) naturally creates a pace break
- Bridge slides are never adjacent to each other

### Step 5: Assemble the judo sequence

Build the slide sequence using the six judo phases from THE VOICE.
Each phase describes the READER'S experience, not the content's role:

1. **CATCH** (1 slide)
   - Hook exploits the groove. Judo catches scrolling momentum.
   - The reader's existing belief IS the entry point.
   - Lead with a FACT or NUMBER, not a claim about the reader.

2. **COMMIT** (1-2 slides)
   - Lock confirms the groove. Reader nods. Seeds planted.
   - The reader deepens their commitment to their current model.
   - Last COMMIT slide contains the first micro-crack — the
     inoculation that will bloom later.

3. **REDIRECT** (2-3 slides)
   - "But that's not what happened." Turns their momentum.
   - Evidence that breaks the prediction the reader just committed to.
   - The reader's own conviction provides the force for the turn.
   - 1 Full slide per evidence cluster. Bridge slides between
     clusters for pacing.

4. **THROW** (1-2 slides)
   - Reader discovers the insight THEMSELVES. Magician moment.
   - This is the crossbar fact — the discovery, not a statement.
   - You don't state the conclusion. The evidence makes the
     reader's own cognition produce it.

5. **LAND** (1 slide)
   - Every earlier slide means something different now. Satisfaction.
   - The reader looks back and sees the whole judo match.
   - Concrete and specific — not abstract ("think about it").

6. **LOOP** (1 slide)
   - Ending IS the hook at a different altitude. Save/share.
   - Matchbook: reveals the carousel's own mechanism.
   - Creates desire for the blog, not satisfaction from the carousel.

### Step 6: Verify the arc

Read the sequence. Does each slide EARN the next swipe? Where does
momentum die? Where does the reader think "I've seen enough"?

If any slide has zero belief shift, cut it. The derivative is zero —
it's dead weight.

**Judo verification:** Read the CATCH and the LOOP together. Are they
the same statement at different altitudes? If not, the toroid is
broken — the carousel is a line, not a loop.

## Slide Types

Slide types are tools on a shelf. Content picks them up:

- **Full** — evidence, mechanism, narrative (label + stats + body)
- **Bridge** — animation-forward, 2-3 lines text, reinforces visually
- **Pivot** — short dramatic text, emotional turn
- **Crossbar** — headline + body revealing core finding
- **Protocol** — numbered list / methodology
- **Matchbook** — meta-reveal + CTA, self-referential

## Output

```
CATCH: [the groove, stated as reader's prediction]
LOOP: [how the catch resolves at a different altitude]
TOTAL SLIDES: [N — determined by content]

SLIDE 1: [type] [CATCH] [content from 9b] [belief shift]
SLIDE 2: [type] [COMMIT] [content from 9b] [belief shift]
...
SLIDE N: [type] [LOOP] [content from 9b] [belief shift]
```

This output becomes the structural input to THE VOICE (9d), which
collapses the superposition of "what words does the reader experience?"
into judo-throw narrative copy.

## Quality Gate

- CATCH and LOOP are explicitly defined and connected (same statement,
  different altitudes)
- Every slide has a non-zero belief shift
- COMMIT rides the groove (familiar, deepening, seeds planted)
- REDIRECT breaks the prediction (evidence, not argument)
- THROW is a DISCOVERY, not a statement
- LAND provides satisfaction (concrete, not abstract)
- LOOP creates desire for the blog, not satisfaction from the carousel
- No fact appears on multiple slides
- Bridge slides exist between consecutive evidence clusters
- Slide count is justified by content, not prescribed
- Judo phases describe the READER'S experience, not the content's role
