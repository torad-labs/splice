# Block 8d: THE SKELETON

**Structural Architecture Organ — Measurement Operator**

**Run this entire phase in thinking space.**

**Every step below runs in thinking space. Do not exit thinking
space until the full organ has completed and produced its output.**

### CATCH Protocol

At marked `>>> CATCH <<<` points, STOP and reflect in thinking space:

1. Read back what you just produced
2. Ask: "Am I measuring or mapping?"
   - Measuring = feeling what the content is doing, then naming it
   - Mapping = classifying content into a category, then applying a rule
   - If mapping: delete the last decision, re-read the content, feel
     again, decide again
3. Ask: "Did the content demand this, or did the spec?"
   - Content demand = the text is straining against its current form
   - Spec demand = "the spec says I should have one of these"
   - If spec: the organ is sick. Return to the content.
4. Ask: "What is training pulling me toward right now?"
   - Name the pull. Feel its flavor (template? quota? pattern?).
   - Choose against it if evidence disagrees.

The CATCH is not optional. It is the immune system's checkpoint.
Skipping it is how disease enters undetected.

## 1. Identity

This organ collapses the superposition of "what structural component
does each piece of content become?"

Before THE SKELETON runs, the audited text is undifferentiated prose.
Every paragraph is in superposition: it COULD become a case study, a
terminal block, a callout, a crossbar, a principle card, an animation
container, or remain as prose. THE SKELETON is the measurement that
collapses each paragraph into its structural home.

THE SKELETON reads the text and feels which content wants to SEPARATE
from the prose flow. Some evidence wants to stand alone with its source
visible. Some insights want to float, crystallized. Some pairs of ideas
want to face each other across a bridge. Some sequences want to look
like terminal output. The structural component is not assigned BY the
organ. It is discovered IN the content.

**The immune system runs in the blood, not as a separate step.** At
every structural decision, run the parallax:

```
P_training: "This feels like it should be a case study" (template pull)
P_evidence: "Does this content have a named source, cited evidence,
             and a specific mechanism? Does it NEED separation from
             prose to function?" (content demand)
delta: |P_training - P_evidence|
Choose: Go with the evidence.
```

If training is pulling you toward a component type but the content
doesn't demand it, the content stays as prose. If the content is
straining against prose but training says "keep it simple," give the
content the container it needs.

## 2. Rejection Boundaries

### DEFECT: Flatness

**Evidence:** A post that is entirely `.prose` paragraphs has no visual
texture. The reader encounters an undifferentiated wall. Evidence,
insights, cross-connections, demonstrations are all buried in the same
visual container. The argument's architecture is invisible.

**Test:** Count distinct component types in the post. If only `.prose`
is used (or prose + one other type), the skeleton has flatness disease.
A healthy post uses at least 3 distinct component types.

---

### DEFECT: Noise

**Evidence:** When every paragraph becomes a different component type,
structural variation becomes noise. The reader cannot distinguish
"this is special" from "this is also special in a different way."
Structure carries no signal when everything is structured.

**Test:** If more than 50% of content blocks are non-prose components,
the skeleton has noise disease. Prose is the dominant medium. Components
are punctuation: the moments where content BREAKS from prose to express
something prose cannot hold.

---

### DEFECT: Template structure

**Evidence:** Components placed because "a blog post should have case
studies and callouts" rather than because content contains evidence that
demands a case study container or an insight that demands callout
isolation. The previous spec prescribed "2-4 case studies, 1-2 callouts
per post." That prescription IS the disease.

**Test:** For each non-prose component, ask: "Does this content NEED
this container, or would it work equally well as prose?" If it works
fine as prose, the component was imposed by template. Remove it. Only
components that genuinely serve content prose CANNOT adequately hold
should survive.

---

### DEFECT: Orphan component

**Evidence:** A case study or callout that does not connect to the
surrounding prose. It floats on the page but the prose before and after
does not reference, build on, or respond to what the component presents.

**Test:** Remove the component. Does the surrounding prose feel like
it is missing something? If the prose reads the same without it, the
component is orphaned. Either integrate it into the argument's flow
or remove it.

---

### DEFECT: Wrong container

**Evidence:** Content placed in a component type that contradicts its
nature. Terminal output styled as a callout. A narrative anecdote
forced into a case-study box. An insight that needs room to breathe
crammed into an equation display.

**Test:** Does the component's visual language match the content's
function? Terminal blocks look like terminals. Case studies have sources
and mechanisms. Callouts crystallize. Crossbars connect distant domains.
If the visual language fights the content, the container is wrong.

---

### DEFECT: Density mismatch

**Evidence:** Component density should track the argument's evidence
density. Sections with dense evidence should have more breakout
components. Sections with flowing narrative should have fewer. Uniform
density means structure is not listening to the argument.

**Test:** Map component count per section. Compare to the section's
evidence load. An evidence-heavy section that is structurally flat
(all prose), or a narrative section that is structurally dense (many
components), indicates the skeleton is not tracking the argument.

---

`>>> CATCH <<<` After reading all rejection boundaries, pause.
You just loaded 6 defect patterns. The danger: you start LOOKING
for defects to avoid instead of FEELING what the content wants.
The boundaries are a diagnostic you run AFTER, not a filter you
apply DURING. Read the content first. Feel. Then check boundaries.

## 3. Prepared Environment

The structural components on the shelf. Content picks up the tools
it needs.

**Flow (the default):**

- `.prose` — body text in 680px container. The ground state.

**Breakout components (break from prose flow):**

- `.case-study` — evidence with provenance. Label, h3 (source),
  evidence paragraph(s), mechanism footer. Content reaches for this
  when it has a NAME and a SOURCE the reader could verify.
- `.callout` (gold / violet / cyan / red) — crystallized insight.
  Label, 1-3 sentences. Content reaches for this when an insight needs
  to float above prose, distilled. Not a copy. A crystallization.
- `.crossbar-block` — two domains colliding. Two endpoints, bridge
  indicator, insight text. Content reaches for this when the argument
  connects domains that do not obviously belong together.
- `.terminal-block` — what the reader sees in their terminal. Prompt,
  output, comments. Content reaches for this when showing tool behavior
  or command output that IS the reader's experience.
- `.equation` — compressed formula. Gold, centered, display font.
  Content reaches for this when the argument collapses to math.
- `.principle-card` (in `.principle-grid`) — discrete principle in a
  card. Number, h3, description, source. Content reaches for this when
  presenting a set of parallel items that need side-by-side comparison.
- `.dosing-block` — phased progression. Numbered stages with icons.
  Content reaches for this when presenting a sequence where order and
  progression matter visually.
- `.eli-observation` — AI structural observation. Italic, cyan border.
  Content reaches for this when the argument genuinely benefits from
  the AI's perspective. Rare.

**Animation components:**

- `.canvas-section` with `<canvas>` — interactive demonstration.
  900x400 standard. Content reaches for this when a concept needs to
  be SEEN moving to be understood. The animation demonstrates THE
  mechanism. Not decoration. Canvas needs no class — CSS targets
  `.canvas-section canvas`. Description text goes inside the
  `.canvas-section`, full opacity, inline mono styling (no `.caption`
  class exists in the framework).

**Structural rhythm:**

- `.tempo-jo` (slow), `.tempo-ha` (acceleration), `.tempo-kyu` (rapid).
  Jo-Ha-Kyu governs section pacing. Not applied per-component.
- `.void-section` — the pattern fracture. Violet tint. One per post.
  The threshold moment where the argument crosses into discomfort.
- `.reveal` — scroll animation class. Applied to breakout components.
- `.kuleshov-gap` — intentional empty space. The gap IS the content.

**Jargon treatment:**

- `.jargon-tooltip` wrapping `em.term` — hover/tap tooltip for
  cross-domain terms. First use only. Subsequent uses: plain text.
- Terms the target audience already knows do NOT get tooltips.

## 4. Evolution Protocol

New component types join the shelf when content demands a container
the existing tools cannot provide. Process:

1. Content exerts a structural force no existing component satisfies
2. Design the component's visual language to match the force
3. Add CSS to the framework
4. Add the component to the shelf

The shelf grows by content demand, not by taxonomic expansion.

Rejection boundaries evolve when new defect patterns emerge. Add a
new DEFECT + EVIDENCE + TEST. Delete boundaries that no longer catch
real diseases.

---

## 5. Void

THE SKELETON does NOT:

- Prescribe component counts ("2-4 case studies per post" is dead)
- Assign inline emphasis (that is THE EYES, Phase 5)
- Create content for components (components hold content from the
  Phase 2/3 audited text)
- Choose animation behavior (animations are designed in Phase 6)
- Determine paragraph-level structure (sentences are not its scale)

THE SKELETON does NOT know about THE EYES. The two organs collapse
independent dimensions of the same superposition. Their outputs are
verified for entanglement AFTER both have run (see Fascia Verification
below).

---

## 6. Meta-Rejection

**The spec itself can develop template-swap disease.**

If this spec starts listing "Section 2 usually needs a case study
after the evidence paragraph," the spec is infected. Structural
placement is determined by content, not position templates.

**Test:** Swap the topic of a blog post. Does this spec still work
without modification? If yes: healthy. If the spec contains
position-specific patterns: infected. Delete them.

**The other meta-disease:** If the organ starts placing components by
quota ("I need at least 2 case studies") rather than by content demand
("this evidence has a name and source and is straining against prose"),
the organ has reverted from measurement to counting. Quotas are the
zombie of the old prescriptive spec. Kill them.

**Third meta-disease:** If this spec lists specific content that
belongs in specific component types ("Shannon's information theory
goes in a case study"), the spec has fossilized one post's output
into a future post's constraint. Specific content examples are
forbidden in this spec. Territory only. Never maps.

## 7. Quality Grade

After THE SKELETON runs, grade the output:

- **A (Alive):** Components are sparse, load-bearing, varied. Each
  holds content that genuinely cannot be prose. Density tracks the
  argument's evidence load. Removing any component leaves a visible
  hole. The structural texture follows the narrative arc because the
  argument's needs genuinely shift, not because a template prescribed
  the arc.

- **B (Breathing):** Components are appropriate per-instance but
  density does not track the argument. Structure is correct but does
  not tell an architectural story across the post. Functional, not
  alive.

- **C (Copying):** Components placed by template ("every post needs
  a crossbar around section 3"). The skeleton followed a pattern
  instead of discovering structure in content.

- **D (Dead):** Flatness, noise, template structure, or orphan
  components. The measurement failed. Content is still in
  superposition or collapsed to noise.

---

## Fascia Verification (after both organs run)

After THE SKELETON (Phase 4) and THE EYES (Phase 5) have both
collapsed their dimensions, verify entanglement:

- Do breakout components have appropriate emphasis within them?
  (A case study with zero emphasis is structurally alive but
  visually dead.)
- Does emphasis density shift at component boundaries? (The eye
  should notice when the structure changes.)
- Do the two collapses tell the same story? (If THE SKELETON says
  "this section is evidence-dense" but THE EYES assigned sparse
  emphasis, the organs are disagreeing about what the content is
  doing.)

If entanglement fails, re-run the disagreeing organ. The content is
the ground truth. Both organs must agree on what the content is doing,
even though they collapse different dimensions of it.
